WHAT IS ASTRONOMY?

Astronomy is the scientific study of celestial objects and phenomena, which include stars, planets, galaxies, nebulae, comets, and other cosmic entities. It is a branch of natural science that deals with the observation, analysis, and interpretation of the universe and its contents.

Astronomy is the study of everything in the universe beyond Earth's atmosphere. That includes objects we can see with our naked eyes, like the Sun , the Moon , the planets, and the stars . It also includes objects we can only see with telescopes or other instruments, like faraway galaxies and tiny particles.

Astronomy has been practiced for thousands of years by different cultures around the world, with significant contributions from ancient civilizations such as the Egyptians, Greeks, and Chinese.

Astronomy Facts and Stats:

  • Our solar system is about 4.5 billion years old.
  • The Moon is drifting away from Earth at a rate of 1.5 inches (3.8 cm) a year.
  • A person who weighs 220 pounds (100 kg) on Earth would weigh only 84 pounds (34 kg) on Mars.
  • Astronomers have made more than 1.4 million observations using the Hubble Space Telescope.
  • Neutron stars rotate at a speed of 600 times per second.
